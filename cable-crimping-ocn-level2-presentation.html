Cable Crimping

Terminate and test an Ethernet cable correctly

OCN NI Level 2 Diploma in Information Technology

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Identify tools and materials for RJ45 termination
  • Follow the T568B colour order accurately
  • Crimp both ends of a straight-through patch lead
  • Test continuity and recognise common faults
  • Work safely with cutters and cable jackets

Why Make Cables?

Custom lengths keep racks tidy and classrooms flexible. A bad crimp causes flaky links that look like “Wi‑Fi problems”.

Tip

Straight-through T568B–T568B is the classroom standard for patch leads.

Tip

Crimping Steps

  1. Measure and cut cable to length; slide boot on if used
  2. Strip ~20–25 mm of outer jacket — don’t nick pairs
  3. Untwist minimally; arrange colours to T568B
  4. Flatten, trim conductors evenly to ~12–14 mm
  5. Insert fully into RJ45 — check colours through the nose
  6. Crimp firmly; repeat other end; test with wire mapper

Test Your Knowledge!

Let's see what you've learned about cable crimping

Q1: A classroom patch lead usually uses…

AT568A one end and coaxial the other
BT568B on both ends (straight-through)
COnly four wires ever
DNo colour standard
Answer: B — Straight-through T568B–T568B is the common patch standard.

Q2: Why use a cable tester after crimping?

AIt charges the cable
BIt checks wire map / continuity before you blame Wi‑Fi
CIt paints the jacket
DTesters are optional decoration
Answer: B — Testing catches opens, shorts and miswires immediately.

Q3: Nicking copper while stripping often causes…

AFaster Gigabit forever
BWeak or intermittent conductors
CAutomatic PoE boost
DBetter Wi‑Fi channels
Answer: B — Damaged conductors fail later under bend stress.
