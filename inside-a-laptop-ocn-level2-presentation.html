Inside a Laptop

Open a serviceable laptop and recognise the parts technicians must know

OCN NI Level 2 — IT fundamentals

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Identify major internal laptop components by sight
  • Explain the role of each key part in a working system
  • Apply ESD and safe disassembly habits
  • Distinguish user-replaceable vs specialised service items
  • Link hardware faults to likely components

Why Open a Laptop?

Support technicians diagnose more than software. Opening a serviceable laptop builds the visual map you need for upgrades, cleaning and fault finding.

Today’s demo: identify parts — not full repair.

RAM

SO-DIMM modules — common upgrade

Storage

Wireless card

Battery

Internal pack — handle carefully

Display & webcam

Panel, hinges, camera, mic

Demo Disassembly Flow

  1. Power off, unplug AC, and use ESD protection
  2. Remove bottom cover screws (map screw lengths — they differ!)
  3. Lift cover gently; watch for clips and ribbon cables
  4. Point out battery, storage, RAM, Wi‑Fi card and cooling path
  5. Show how modules seat and lock (without forcing)
  6. Reassemble carefully — no pinched cables, correct screws

Replaceable vs Specialist

Often field-replaceable

  • RAM (SO-DIMM)
  • M.2 / 2.5" storage
  • Wi‑Fi card
  • Battery (model-dependent)
  • Keyboard / trackpad (some models)

Specialist / fragile

  • Motherboard
  • CPU (often soldered)
  • Display assembly
  • Hinges
  • Liquid damage repair

Fault → Likely Part

Overheating / shutdown

Dust in fans, dried thermal paste

Crashes / blue screens

Faulty or incompatible RAM

Slow boot / freezes

Failing HDD or full SSD

No wireless

Loose antenna or dead Wi‑Fi card

Short runtime

Aged battery or heavy background load

Keys not working

Keyboard ribbon / liquid spill

Q1: A common user upgrade inside many laptops is…

AThe soldered CPU
BRAM and/or storage
CThe motherboard chipset
DThe hinge metal
Answer: B — RAM and SSDs are the most common field upgrades.

Q2: Different screw lengths matter because…

AThey look nicer
BWrong screws can pierce boards or leave covers loose
CLaptops only use one screw type ever
DLonger screws charge the battery
Answer: B — Screw maps prevent damage during reassembly.

Q3: Before opening the cover you should…

AKeep AC power connected
BSkip ESD — laptops are immune
CPower off, unplug and use ESD precautions
DRemove the screen first always
Answer: C — Same safe-working rules as desktops.
