Why Network Design Matters

A good network design isn't just "plug it all in" — it's a set of deliberate decisions about devices, addressing, security and cabling that keep a business running reliably and safely.

Presenting to Stakeholders

Pitch tips

Business owners aren't engineers. Lead with benefits (reliable, secure, room to grow), back it with a simple diagram, and keep jargon to a minimum.

Do

  • Show a clear, labelled diagram
  • Explain the "why" behind each choice
  • Mention rough cost and any risks covered
  • Keep it to time — practise the pitch

Avoid

  • Reading every IP address aloud
  • Unexplained acronyms (VLAN, DHCP, NAT)
  • A design with no security mentioned
  • Running over the 3-minute slot

Practical Task: Design & Pitch

  1. Form teams and read the brief: a 6-person business needs a working office network
  2. List every device required (router, switch, AP, printer, NAS, PCs, laptops)
  3. Draw the topology on paper or in draw.io — label every device
  4. Write an IP addressing plan: gateway, static devices, DHCP range, guest Wi-Fi
  5. Add at least three security measures (firewall rules, guest isolation, strong passwords, backups)
  6. Note the cabling approach: cable type, rough cable runs, any patch panel
  7. Prepare a 3-minute pitch: problem, design, security, cost, benefits
  8. Present to the class as if presenting to the business owner
  9. Take questions and note any improvements suggested

Test Your Knowledge!

Let's see what you've learned about network design

Q1: Why should guest Wi-Fi be separated from the staff LAN?

AIt makes the Wi-Fi faster for everyone
BIt stops visitors reaching internal devices and files
CIt's required to connect a printer
DIt reduces the router's cost
Answer: B — Isolating guest traffic protects internal systems and data.

Q2: Which device should typically have a fixed (static) IP?

AThe shared network printer or server
BA visitor's phone
CAny laptop that changes desks daily
DA device that is never turned on
Answer: A — Shared, always-on resources like servers and printers work best on a fixed address.

Q3: When pitching a design to a business owner, you should…

ARead out every configuration line
BLead with benefits and explain choices simply
CAvoid mentioning security at all
DSkip the diagram to save time
Answer: B — Stakeholders respond to clear benefits and simple, well-explained diagrams.
