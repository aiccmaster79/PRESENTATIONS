Deepfakes & Misinformation

Can You Trust What You See?

OCN NI Level 2 Diploma in Information Technology

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Define a deepfake and explain how AI-generated media is made at a high level
  • Apply verification techniques to check whether content is genuine
  • Describe the impact of deepfakes and misinformation on individuals and society
  • Compare real and AI-generated media critically
  • Build a personal checklist for verifying content online

What Is a Deepfake?

Definition

A deepfake is AI-generated video, image, or audio that makes a real person appear to say or do something they never actually did.

Not all AI media is malicious

The same generative technology powers film effects, dubbing, and accessibility tools — the risk comes from deceptive use, not the technology alone.

How Deepfakes Are Made (High Level)

1. Collect real photos/video/audio of a person

↓

2. Train a generative AI model on that data

↓

3. Model generates new, synthetic footage

↓

4. Fake content is edited, polished and shared

The more real source material available (photos, speeches, interviews), the more convincing the fake — which is why public figures are common targets.

Verification Techniques

Check the source

Is it from a trusted, named outlet or an anonymous account?

Reverse image search

See where else the image/video has appeared online

Look for artefacts

Odd blinking, lighting, blurring or lip-sync mismatches

Cross-check outlets

Are other trustworthy sources reporting the same thing?

Check the date

Old footage is often reshared as if it were current

Pause before sharing

Strong emotional reactions are a red flag — verify first

Impact on Society

Risks

  • Political misinformation and election interference
  • Scams using cloned voices of family members
  • Reputational harm and harassment
  • Erosion of trust in genuine video/audio evidence

Legitimate Uses

  • Film dubbing and special effects
  • Accessibility (voice restoration for disabled users)
  • Education and training simulations
  • Clearly labelled satire and art

Practical Task

Real or Fake? Build a Verification Checklist

As a class, compare curated real vs AI-generated media and vote before building a shared checklist.

  1. View a series of paired images/audio clips — some real, some AI-generated
  2. Vote individually on each: real or fake?
  3. Reveal the answers and discuss what clues gave the fakes away
  4. As a group, agree on a 5-point checklist for verifying online content
  5. Test your checklist on one final, unseen example

Kit Needed

Curated example media only — use publicly documented, labelled examples for teaching, never create deceptive content of real people.

Test Your Knowledge!

Q1: What is a deepfake?

AA very high-resolution photograph
BAI-generated media that makes someone appear to say or do something they didn't
CA type of computer virus
DA backup copy of a video file
Answer: B — Deepfakes use generative AI trained on real footage to fabricate convincing fake video or audio.

Q2: Which is a good first step to verify suspicious content?

AShare it immediately so others can see it
BCheck the source and cross-check with trusted outlets
CAssume it's true because it looks realistic
DIgnore the date it was posted
Answer: B — Checking the source and cross-referencing trusted outlets helps confirm whether content is genuine.

Q3: Which is a genuine societal risk of deepfakes?

AThey always make devices run faster
BThey can spread political misinformation and enable scams
CThey automatically fix spelling mistakes
DThey cannot be used to impersonate anyone
Answer: B — Deepfakes are increasingly used for scams, harassment, and misinformation, which is why verification skills matter.

Key Takeaways

Deepfakes Are Convincing

Generative AI can fabricate realistic video, images and audio

Verify Before You Trust

Source, cross-checks and artefacts reveal most fakes

Real Societal Impact

Misinformation affects politics, safety and trust in media

Build a Habit

A simple personal checklist makes verification routine
