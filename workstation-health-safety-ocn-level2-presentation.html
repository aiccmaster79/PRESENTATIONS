Safe Setup

Ergonomics, health & safety at the workstation

OCN NI Level 2 Diploma in Information Technology

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Set up a screen, chair and desk at correct ergonomic heights
  • Identify cable and trip hazards in a workstation
  • Recognise early signs of RSI and eye strain
  • Explain the basics of PAT testing for electrical equipment
  • Audit your own desk against a health & safety checklist

Why Ergonomics Matters

Most IT jobs mean hours at a screen every day. A poorly set-up workstation causes pain, injury and lost productivity — good habits now protect your health for a whole career.

Prevents Injury

Reduces risk of RSI, back and neck pain

Protects Eyesight

Cuts eye strain and headaches

Boosts Focus

Comfortable posture = better concentration

It's the Law

Employers must assess display screen equipment risks

Setting Up Your Screen & Seat

Screen height

Top of the screen roughly at eye level

Viewing distance

An arm's length away, roughly 50–70cm

Seating posture

Feet flat, back supported, knees at ~90°

Keyboard & mouse

Wrists straight, elbows close to the body

Cables, Lighting & Electrical Safety

Good Practice

  • Cables tidied away from walkways
  • Blinds/lamps used to avoid screen glare
  • Plugs and leads checked and PAT tested
  • No overloaded extension sockets

Common Hazards

  • Trailing cables across a walkway
  • Screen facing a bright window (glare)
  • Frayed or damaged power leads
  • Daisy-chained extension leads

RSI & Eye Strain — Spotting the Signs

Take it seriously

Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) and digital eye strain build up gradually. Catching the early signs and adjusting your setup prevents long-term damage.

RSI Signs

Aching wrists, tingling fingers, stiff shoulders

Eye Strain Signs

Headaches, blurred vision, dry or tired eyes

20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 min, look 20ft away for 20 seconds

Practical Task

Audit Your Own Workstation

Using a health & safety checklist, assess your own desk (in class or at home) and make one real improvement.

1. Check

Score your desk against screen height, seating, cables and lighting

2. Identify

Pick the single biggest hazard or discomfort risk

3. Fix it

Make one practical change — raise a monitor, tidy a cable

4. Photograph

Capture a before/after photo of your fix for your portfolio

Test Your Knowledge!

Let's see what you've learned about workstation safety

Q1: Where should the top of your monitor roughly sit?

AWell above your head
BAt eye level
COn the floor
DIt doesn't matter
Answer: B — Eye-level screen height keeps your neck in a neutral, comfortable position.

Q2: What does PAT testing check?

AElectrical safety of portable appliances
BHow fast your typing is
CWi-Fi signal strength
DYour chair's comfort level
Answer: A — Portable Appliance Testing checks that electrical equipment is safe to use.

Q3: Which of these is a common cause of RSI?

ADrinking enough water
BPoor posture and repetitive strain
CTaking regular breaks
DUsing a supportive chair
Answer: B — Repeated movements combined with poor posture are the leading cause of RSI.
