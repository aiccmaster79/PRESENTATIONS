T568B Colour Order

Hold the RJ45 clip down, contacts facing you. Pin 1 is on the left:

PinT568B ColourPair
1White/OrangePair 2
2OrangePair 2
3White/GreenPair 3
4BluePair 1
5White/BluePair 1
6GreenPair 3
7White/BrownPair 4
8BrownPair 4

Memory tip

B for Business-standard patch leads in many UK/EU installs — keep both ends identical for straight-through cables.

Step-by-Step Termination

  1. Measure and cut the cable to length
  2. Strip ~20–25 mm of outer jacket (don’t nick wires)
  3. Untwist pairs minimally; arrange into T568B order
  4. Flatten, trim conductors evenly (~12–13 mm exposed)
  5. Slide wires fully into RJ45—jacket sits inside the plug
  6. Crimp firmly; repeat T568B on the other end
  7. Test with a cable tester before installing

Straight-Through vs Crossover

Straight-Through (common)

  • Same standard both ends (usually T568B)
  • PC ↔ switch / router
  • Most patch leads you will make
  • Auto-MDI-X often makes crossover unnecessary

Crossover (legacy)

  • T568A one end, T568B the other
  • Historically PC ↔ PC or switch ↔ switch
  • Rare on modern gigabit gear
  • Know it for exams / older kit

Common Faults

Wrong colour order

Opens / miswires — no link or errors

Nicked conductors

Intermittent faults after bending

Uneven trim

Wires don’t seat on gold contacts

Jacket not in plug

Poor strain relief — fails with tugs

Untwisted too far

Crosstalk / fails higher category tests

Skipped testing

Problems only found after installation

Q1: On T568B, pins 1 and 2 are…

AWhite/Green and Green
BWhite/Orange and Orange
CBlue and White/Blue
DBrown and White/Brown
Answer: B — T568B starts with the orange pair on pins 1–2.

Q2: For a normal PC-to-switch patch lead you should…

AUse opposite standards on each end
BUse the same standard (e.g. T568B) on both ends
CLeave two wires out
DSkip crimping
Answer: B — Straight-through cables use identical wiring both ends.

Q3: Why use a cable tester?

ATo charge phones
BTo verify continuity and correct pin mapping
CTo install Windows
DTo change Wi‑Fi passwords
Answer: B — Testers catch opens, shorts and miswires before go-live.
