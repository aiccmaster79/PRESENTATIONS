What is Quantum Computing?

Definition

Quantum computing uses the rules of quantum physics to process information in ways that classical computers cannot—especially for certain complex problems.

Think of it like this...

A classical computer checks paths one by one. For some puzzles, a quantum computer can explore many possibilities at once—like trying many routes through a maze in parallel.

Classical vs Quantum

Classical Computing

  • Uses bits: either 0 or 1
  • Excellent for everyday tasks
  • Predictable and mature
  • Runs phones, PCs, servers
  • Struggles with huge search/optimisation problems

Quantum Computing

  • Uses qubits with quantum states
  • Built for specialised hard problems
  • Still early and experimental
  • Accessed mainly via the cloud
  • Not a replacement for your laptop

Bits vs Qubits

Classical Bit

One State

A bit is like a light switch—fully OFF (0) or ON (1). Nothing in between.

Binary: 0 or 1 only

Qubit

Richer State

A qubit can hold a combination of 0 and 1 until measured—then it becomes 0 or 1.

Quantum unit of info

Why It Matters

More Power (for some tasks)

With more qubits working together, certain calculations scale differently than on classical machines.

Not faster at everything

Superposition (Simplified)

Superposition means a qubit can be in a blend of states (a mix of 0 and 1) before you look at it.

Before Measure

Superposition

0 or 1 Result

Spinning Coin Analogy

While a coin spins, it isn't clearly heads or tails. When it lands (measurement), you get one definite answer.

Entanglement (Simplified)

Linked Qubits

Entanglement links qubits so that knowing something about one can tell you about the other—even when they are treated as a pair in a computation.

Work as a Team

Entangled qubits share correlated behaviour useful in algorithms

Key Resource

Many quantum speed-ups rely on entanglement plus clever circuits

Fragile

Noise and heat can destroy entanglement quickly

Reality Check: NISQ & Cloud

Today we are in the NISQ era — Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum. Machines have limited qubits and make errors easily.

Noisy

Qubits lose information quickly; error correction is still hard.

Intermediate

Dozens to hundreds of qubits—not yet huge fault-tolerant systems.

Myths vs Facts

Myths

  • Quantum will replace all PCs next year
  • It is infinitely fast at everything
  • Only genius physicists can understand any of it
  • Businesses must buy hardware today

Facts

  • Classical computers remain essential
  • Advantage is for specific problem types
  • Basic concepts are teachable at Level 2
  • Cloud access and research partnerships are enough for now

Q1: How is a qubit different from a classical bit?

AA qubit only stores text documents
BA qubit can represent a combination of 0 and 1 until measured
CA qubit is just a faster USB drive
DThere is no difference
Answer: B - Qubits use quantum states; measurement gives a classical 0 or 1.

Q2: What does NISQ stand for?

ANew Internet Security Quotient
BNetwork Integrated Server Query
CNoisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum
DNational IT Skills Qualification
Answer: C - Today's quantum devices are noisy and mid-scale.

Q3: Which statement about quantum computing today is most accurate?

AIt is specialised, often cloud-accessed, and not a laptop replacement
BEvery home already has a quantum PC
CIt only works offline with no software
DIt has made classical computers obsolete
Answer: A - Quantum is emerging, specialised and commonly used via the cloud.
