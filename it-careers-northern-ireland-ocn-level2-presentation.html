Practical Task

Map Roles, Pick One, Plan Ahead

Map five IT roles to the skills and NI-relevant local routes that lead to them, then choose one and write a mini action plan.

  1. List the five roles: support, networking, cyber, dev, data
  2. For each, note two key skills it needs
  3. For each, research one NI-relevant apprenticeship, college course or employer sector
  4. Choose the one role that interests you most
  5. Write a mini action plan: one skill to build, one qualification to research, one thing to do this term
  6. Share your plan with a partner and compare choices

Q1: Which role focuses on protecting systems from attacks?

AData Analyst
BCyber Security Analyst
CGraphic Designer
DReceptionist
Answer: B — Cyber security analysts monitor, detect and defend against threats.

Q2: An apprenticeship lets you…

AOnly study, with no work involved
BEarn a wage while training and gaining a qualification
CSkip all learning entirely
DWork for free indefinitely
Answer: B — Apprenticeships combine paid work with structured training toward a real qualification.

Q3: A good first step in a personal development plan is…

AWait for opportunities to appear on their own
BIdentify one skill to build and a concrete next step
CDo nothing until you graduate
DAvoid asking anyone for help
Answer: B — A specific, actionable goal is far more effective than a vague intention.
