What is Biotechnology?

Biotechnology uses living systems — cells, genes, microbes — and modern tools to solve problems in health, food, energy and the environment.

Red Biotech

Medicine & health — vaccines, therapies, diagnostics

Green Biotech

Agriculture — crops, livestock, pest resistance

White Biotech

Industry — enzymes, biofuels, sustainable materials

Why it matters for this unit

Biotech is emerging because tools like CRISPR are new, fast-changing, and reshaping society and business — just like AI, IoT and 5G.

What is CRISPR-Cas9?

CRISPR-Cas9 is a precise gene-editing tool. Scientists can find a specific DNA sequence and cut it — then repair, disable or rewrite that gene.

CRISPR

  • Acts like a GPS for DNA
  • Uses a guide RNA to find the target
  • Originally discovered in bacteria

Cas9

  • Acts like molecular scissors
  • Cuts DNA at the guided location
  • Cell repairs the cut — or we insert new DNA

CRISPR Explained Simply

1. Guide finds gene

2. Cas9 cuts DNA

3. Edit & repair

Think of it like this...

Imagine DNA as a long book. CRISPR finds the exact sentence you want to change, cuts it out, and lets you fix a typo — or write a new sentence.

Cancer Research

Engineering immune cells to better recognise and fight tumours

Drug Discovery

Faster lab models to test how genes affect disease and treatments

Diagnostics

CRISPR-based tests that detect viruses and pathogens quickly

Personalised Medicine

Treatments tailored to a patient's genetic profile

Clinical Trials

Applications: Agriculture

Disease-resistant and drought-tolerant plants for food security

Pest Resistance

Crops with improved vitamins, flavour or shelf life

Climate Adaptation

Help farming cope with changing weather and soil conditions

Livestock Health

Research into disease resistance in animals

Regulation Matters

Rules differ by country — gene-edited food is debated worldwide

The IT Connection

CRISPR is biology — but modern biotech runs on IT infrastructure.

Data & Genomics

DNA sequencing creates huge datasets. Labs need storage, databases and pipelines to analyse genomes.

Bioinformatics, genome browsers

AI & Cloud

Machine learning predicts guide RNA targets; cloud compute trains models and shares research securely.

AWS/Azure for research, ML design tools

Security

Genetic data is highly sensitive. Access control, encryption and compliance protect patients and IP.

GDPR, lab cybersecurity

Ethics & Society

Opportunities

  • Treat previously untreatable diseases
  • Improve food security and nutrition
  • Speed up medical research
  • Reduce environmental impact of farming

Concerns

  • Who decides what gets edited?
  • Unequal access — rich vs poor countries
  • Germline edits affect future generations
  • "Designer" traits and unintended harm

Assessment tip

For distinction-level answers, weigh benefits vs risks and link ethics to regulation, public trust and business decisions.

Case Study: Sickle Cell Therapy

CRISPR in the Clinic

The Challenge

  • Sickle cell disease is genetic and painful
  • Lifelong care is expensive and limited
  • Traditional treatments manage symptoms

The CRISPR Approach

  • Edit patient's own blood stem cells
  • Restore healthier haemoglobin production
  • IT systems track trials, consent & data

Lessons for Emerging Tech

Success depends on science and data security, regulation, cost, skills and public trust — the same adoption factors as other emerging technologies.

Test Your Knowledge!

Q1: What does CRISPR-Cas9 mainly do?

ASpeed up internet connections
BFind and cut specific DNA sequences for gene editing
CEncrypt patient medical records
DReplace the need for cloud computing
Answer: B - CRISPR guides Cas9 to a DNA target so scientists can edit genes.

Q2: Which is a clear IT link to modern biotech?

ABiotech never uses computers
BCRISPR removes the need for data storage
CGenomic data, AI and cloud power research and security
DOnly farmers use CRISPR, not labs
Answer: C - Sequencing, AI target design, cloud compute and cyber security are essential.

Q3: A major ethical concern with CRISPR is:

AIt only works on plants
BEditing heritable traits and unequal access to treatments
CIt cannot treat any disease
DIt makes computers slower
Answer: B - Germline edits and fairness/access raise serious social and ethical questions.
