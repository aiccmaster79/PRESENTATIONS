Why Green IT Matters

Green IT means designing, using and disposing of technology in ways that reduce energy use, waste and environmental harm.

The E-Waste Problem

The Problem

  • Old devices often go straight to landfill
  • Contain toxic materials (lead, mercury, flame retardants)
  • Valuable metals (gold, copper, lithium) go to waste
  • Fast upgrade cycles increase how much e-waste is created

Why It's Fixable

  • Most components can be safely recovered and reused
  • WEEE recycling points exist across Northern Ireland
  • Devices can often be refurbished, not just recycled
  • Data can be securely wiped before reuse

Sustainable IT Practices

Power settings

Enable sleep/standby modes and turn off idle devices

Extend device life

Repair and upgrade rather than replacing early

Buy energy-efficient

Check energy ratings before purchasing new kit

Efficient cloud use

Shared cloud servers can be more efficient than many idle local machines

Refurbish & donate

Working devices can get a second life with new users

Recycle responsibly

Use certified WEEE recycling points for end-of-life kit

Practical Task

Audit, Calculate, and Plan a Recycling Route

In teams, audit the room's devices, estimate their yearly cost, and plan where old kit should go.

  1. List every powered device in the room and note its typical wattage
  2. Estimate hours of use per day and calculate rough daily energy use (watts × hours)
  3. Scale up to a rough yearly electricity cost using a typical unit price
  4. Identify any devices that are old, broken, or due for replacement
  5. Plan a responsible disposal route for that old kit: wipe data → refurbish/donate → WEEE recycling

Kit Needed

Power ratings for classroom devices (nameplate/spec sheet) and a spreadsheet or calculator.

Test Your Knowledge!

Let's see what you've learned about Green IT and e-waste

Q1: What does 'Green IT' mean?

AOnly buying devices that are coloured green
BDesigning, using and disposing of technology to reduce environmental impact
CNever using electricity at all
DA brand of laptop
Answer: B — Green IT is about reducing energy use, waste and environmental harm across a device's whole life.

Q2: What is a major problem with e-waste?

AIt contains toxic materials and is often not recycled properly
BIt takes up no space at all
CIt has no environmental impact
DIt is always recycled by default everywhere
Answer: A — E-waste can contain toxic substances and much of it worldwide is not formally, safely recycled.

Q3: What should happen to a working device that's no longer needed?

AThrow it straight in general waste
BLeave it switched on forever, unused
CSecurely wipe the data, then refurbish/donate it if possible
DSmash it up for fun
Answer: C — Wiping data safely and passing on a working device for reuse is more sustainable than binning it.
