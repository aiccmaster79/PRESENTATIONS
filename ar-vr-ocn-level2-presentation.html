What is Augmented Reality?

Definition — AR

Augmented Reality overlays digital information—images, text, 3D models—onto the real world using a phone, tablet or AR glasses.

Think of it like this...

You still see the real room, but a digital arrow, product preview or label appears on top of it—like a live filter for reality.

What is Virtual Reality?

Definition — VR

Virtual Reality places you inside a fully computer-generated environment, usually with a headset that blocks out the real world.

Think of it like this...

Instead of looking at a screen, you step into a digital world—training rooms, games, museums or simulated workplaces.

AR vs VR Compared

Augmented Reality

  • Real world stays visible
  • Digital content is overlaid
  • Often phone/tablet based
  • Great for guidance & shopping
  • Users stay aware of surroundings
  • Examples: Pokémon GO, IKEA Place

Virtual Reality

  • Fully digital environment
  • Headset immerses the user
  • Controllers for interaction
  • Great for simulation & training
  • Real world is blocked out
  • Examples: Meta Quest, HTC Vive

Benefits & Barriers

Benefits

  • Safer, cheaper practice for high-risk tasks
  • Higher engagement in learning
  • Better product visualisation for customers
  • Remote collaboration possibilities
  • Memorable, immersive experiences

Barriers

  • Hardware cost and setup time
  • Motion sickness for some users
  • Content creation skills needed
  • Privacy and data collection concerns
  • Accessibility and space requirements

Case Study: Hospital Training

Regional Training Centre

Before

  • Limited access to simulation labs
  • High cost of physical manikins
  • Rare emergency drills hard to schedule
  • Inconsistent practice opportunities

After VR

  • VR emergency scenarios on demand
  • Shared headset pool for cohorts
  • Performance scores tracked digitally
  • Staff rehearse without disrupting wards

Result

Faster onboarding, more practice hours per learner, and improved confidence scores before live placements.

Q1: What is the key difference between AR and VR?

AAR overlays digital content on the real world; VR immerses you in a fully virtual world
BAR only works on PCs; VR only works on phones
CThere is no difference—they are the same
DVR overlays labels; AR blocks out reality
Answer: A - AR adds to reality; VR replaces it with a digital environment.

Q2: Which is a typical AR use case?

ABlocking out the real world with a closed headset only
BPreviewing furniture in your living room with a phone app
CWriting spreadsheets with no graphics
DPrinting paper posters only
Answer: B - Retail AR overlays products onto your real space.

Q3: Which is a common barrier to adopting VR in organisations?

AToo many free headsets for everyone
BVR never needs any content
CHardware cost, motion sickness and content creation effort
DIt only works without electricity
Answer: C - Cost, comfort and content skills are typical barriers.
