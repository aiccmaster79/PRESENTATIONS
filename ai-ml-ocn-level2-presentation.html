What is Artificial Intelligence?

Definition

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is technology that enables computers to perform tasks that normally need human intelligence—such as recognising speech, making decisions, or solving problems.

Think of it like this...

AI is the big idea—building machines that can "think" or act smart. Machine Learning is one of the main ways we make that happen.

What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of AI where systems learn from data instead of being programmed with every rule by hand.

Types of Machine Learning

Supervised

Learn with Labels

The model is trained on examples that already have the correct answers (labels).

Spam filters, price prediction

Unsupervised

Find Hidden Patterns

No labels given—the model groups similar data and discovers structure on its own.

Customer segments, anomaly detection

Generative

Create New Content

Models learn patterns then generate text, images, code, or audio that looks original.

ChatGPT, image generators

Risks, Challenges & Ethics

Risks & Challenges

  • Bias in training data can lead to unfair outcomes
  • Privacy concerns when personal data is used
  • Job roles may change or disappear
  • Models can make mistakes with high confidence
  • Deepfakes and misinformation risks

Ethical Principles

  • Be transparent about when AI is used
  • Protect personal data (GDPR)
  • Keep humans in the loop for big decisions
  • Test for bias and unfair treatment
  • Use AI responsibly and legally

Real-World Example

Case Study: Online Retailer Uses ML

Challenge

  • Customers struggled to find products
  • High return rates from poor matches
  • Support team overloaded with FAQs
  • Fraudulent orders slipping through

Solution

  • Recommendation engine (supervised ML)
  • Chatbot for common queries
  • Fraud model flags unusual payments
  • Staff review high-risk cases only

Result

Sales up 18%, support tickets down 40%, and fraud losses cut in half—while keeping humans responsible for final decisions.
