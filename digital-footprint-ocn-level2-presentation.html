What is a Digital Footprint?

Definition

Your digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind — posts, photos, likes, comments, locations and even things others post about you.

Active Footprint

  • Posts, photos and comments you share
  • Profile details you fill in yourself
  • Things you deliberately publish

Passive Footprint

  • Location data and browsing history
  • Data collected by apps and websites
  • Tags and mentions from other people

Public vs Private Information

What strangers can find depends heavily on your settings and habits.

Public by Default

Profile picture, username, and often your bio on many platforms

Often Overlooked

Location tags, school/employer, tagged photos from friends

Should Stay Private

Home address, daily routine, holiday dates, phone number

Risks of Oversharing

A few small, seemingly harmless posts can add up to a complete picture of your life for a stranger.

Burglary Risk

Posting "on holiday!" tells people your home is empty

Identity Theft

Birthdate, school and pet names can answer security questions

Employability

Employers often check public profiles before hiring

Stalking / Harassment

Location tags reveal routines and whereabouts

Protect

Guard Your Own Data

Review settings regularly as platforms change

Practical Task

What Can Strangers Find?

  • Using a made-up persona (never real personal details), map what a stranger could learn from a typical public profile
  • List every piece of information found: name, location clues, routine, workplace/school
  • Discuss as a group how this information could be misused
  • Choose one real account and live-review its privacy settings
  • Lock down at least three settings and note what changed

Stay Safe

Only use a fictional persona or your own account for the search exercise — never research another real classmate without consent.

Q1: What is a digital footprint?

AA type of computer virus
BThe trail of data you leave behind online
CA password manager feature
DA setting in your router
Answer: B - Your footprint includes everything you post, share, and that others post about you.

Q2: Why is posting "on holiday!" with your location risky?

AIt uses too much data
BIt tells people your home is currently empty
CIt slows down your phone
DIt automatically deletes your account
Answer: B - Real-time location posts can signal to strangers that your home is unoccupied.

Q3: Which is good digital citizenship practice?

ATagging friends in photos without asking
BAsking permission before posting photos of others
CSharing your full daily routine publicly
DLeaving every account set to public forever
Answer: B - Respecting others' consent and privacy is central to responsible digital citizenship.
