Ethics & Legal First

Metasploit is a powerful security testing toolkit. Using it on systems without written permission can be illegal and lead to serious consequences.

Classroom rule

We learn Metasploit concepts to understand attacks and improve defences—only on authorised lab targets (e.g. intentionally vulnerable VMs).

What is Metasploit?

Definition

Metasploit is an open-source penetration testing framework (maintained by Rapid7). It helps security professionals check whether systems have known weaknesses—and prove the risk in a controlled way.

Think of it like this...

If a garage has a big toolbox for diagnosing cars, Metasploit is a toolbox for diagnosing security—with many specialised tools organised for testing (not for joyriding).

Who Uses It?

Penetration Testers

Authorised “ethical hackers” hired to find weaknesses

Red Teams

Simulate real attackers against a company’s controls

Blue Teams / SOC

Study attack techniques to detect and block them

Students & Labs

Practise on vulnerable VMs in supervised environments

How It’s Used (High Level)

Permission

Recon

Match Flaw

Report

  1. Scope & authorisation — written approval, clear targets
  2. Information gathering — hosts, services, versions (often with other tools too)
  3. Vulnerability matching — choose relevant modules for known issues
  4. Controlled validation — confirm risk on approved lab/systems
  5. Reporting & remediation — help the organisation patch and harden

Benefits vs Misuse Risks

Legitimate Value

  • Finds real weaknesses before criminals do
  • Standardises testing workflows
  • Supports evidence-based reports
  • Trains defenders on attacker techniques
  • Widely used in industry and education

Misuse Harms

  • Unauthorised access is a crime
  • Can damage systems if used carelessly
  • Violates school / employer policies
  • Destroys trust and career prospects
  • May breach computer misuse laws

Defensive Takeaways

Patch promptly

Many Metasploit modules target known, fixable flaws

Reduce exposure

Firewalls, least privilege, disable unused services

Monitor & detect

Logs and EDR help spot suspicious activity

Authorised testing

Hire/permit testing under clear rules of engagement

Lab Example (Safe)

Intentionally Vulnerable VM

In class, learners may use Metasploit against a purpose-built vulnerable virtual machine on an isolated lab network—never against school production systems or the public internet.

Goal

Understand how unpatched software becomes exploitable—and why updates, hardening and monitoring matter.
