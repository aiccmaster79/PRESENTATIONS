What Is the Internet of Things?

Definition

The Internet of Things (IoT) is the network of everyday physical objects — thermostats, lights, locks, speakers — that connect to the internet to send data and be controlled remotely.

Everyday example

A smart thermostat senses the room temperature, sends that data to an app, and lets you change the heating from your phone — anywhere in the world.

Most consumer devices don't talk to your phone directly — data usually travels out to the cloud and back again, which is where privacy and reliability trade-offs come in.

Benefits vs Risks of a Connected Home

Benefits

  • Convenience — control the home from anywhere
  • Energy savings from automated heating/lighting
  • Extra security through cameras and alerts
  • Independent living support for elderly/disabled users

Risks

  • Weak default passwords across many devices
  • Personal data collected and sold by manufacturers
  • Devices go offline if the internet or cloud service fails
  • Older devices stop receiving security updates

Practical Task

Design a Smart Classroom or Home

In teams, design a smart room from scratch and think through its network and its risks.

  1. Choose a room (classroom, bedroom, kitchen) and pick 5 sensors or devices to install
  2. Draw the network: device → hub/router → cloud → app, showing how each device connects
  3. For each device, write one realistic security risk
  4. Suggest one mitigation per risk (e.g. strong unique password, firmware updates, guest Wi-Fi network)
  5. Present your design to the class in 2 minutes

Kit Needed

Paper or whiteboard for diagrams (a micro:bit can be used for a live temperature-sensor demo if available).

Test Your Knowledge!

Let's see what you've learned about smart homes and IoT

Q1: What does IoT stand for?

AInternet of Things
BInput/Output Technology
CInternal Office Tools
DIntegrated Online Terminal
Answer: A — IoT (Internet of Things) describes everyday physical objects connected to the internet.

Q2: In a typical smart home setup, where does device data usually travel before reaching your phone app?

ADirectly from the device to your phone with no other stop
BThrough the manufacturer's cloud server
CIt never leaves the device
DThrough a fax machine
Answer: B — Most consumer IoT devices send data to a cloud server, which your phone app then reads from.

Q3: Which is a realistic security risk for a smart camera or video doorbell?

AIt uses too much paper
BIts feed or account could be hijacked, exposing your privacy
CIt cannot ever be hacked
DIt only works with a landline
Answer: B — Cameras and doorbells stream sensitive footage, so a hijacked account or weak password is a real privacy risk.
