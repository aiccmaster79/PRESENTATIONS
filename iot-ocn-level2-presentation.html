What is the Internet of Things?

Definition

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical devices—sensors, appliances, vehicles and machines—that collect data, connect to the internet, and can act or be controlled remotely.

Think of it like this...

Your phone is smart because it connects to the internet. IoT extends that idea to everyday objects—thermostats, fridges, factory machines, traffic lights—so they can sense, share and respond.

Why IoT Matters

Billions of devices are already online—IoT is reshaping how homes, businesses and cities monitor and automate the physical world.

The Four-Step Loop

  • Sense — devices measure temperature, motion, location, etc.
  • Connect — data travels via Wi-Fi, cellular, Bluetooth or LPWAN
  • Analyse — cloud or edge systems process the data
  • Act — alerts, automation or control commands are sent back

Sensors, Network & Cloud

Sensors

The Eyes & Ears

Collect physical data: heat, light, pressure, GPS, cameras and more.

Motion PIR, RFID, accelerometers

Network

The Pathways

Moves data from devices to systems—short range or long range.

Wi-Fi, 5G, Zigbee, LoRaWAN

Cloud & Action

The Brain

Stores and analyses data, then triggers alerts or device control.

Dashboards, apps, automation rules

Security & Privacy Risks

Weak Defaults

Default passwords and unpatched firmware are easy targets

Privacy Concerns

Cameras, microphones and location data can be misused

Botnets

Hacked devices can be used to launch large attacks

Supply Chain

Insecure components or cloud apps create hidden risks

Stay Safer

Change default passwords, update firmware, use strong Wi-Fi, and only share the data you really need.

Case Study: Smart Warehouse

Mid-Size Logistics Company

Before

  • Manual stock counts every week
  • Forklift collisions and near-misses
  • Cold-store temperatures checked by clipboard
  • Delayed discovery of broken equipment

After IoT

  • RFID tags for live inventory
  • Proximity sensors for forklift safety
  • Cloud alerts if cold rooms drift
  • Vibration sensors for predictive maintenance

Result

30% fewer stock errors, faster picking, and early warnings that prevented two costly freezer failures.

Q1: What does IoT primarily connect?

AOnly smartphones and laptops
BPhysical devices with sensors that share data over a network
CPrinters in a single office only
DSocial media accounts
Answer: B - IoT links physical devices that sense, connect and often act.

Q2: Which sequence best describes a typical IoT flow?

ASense → Network → Cloud/Analyse → Action
BAction → Print → Delete → Restart
CCloud → Ignore sensors → Manual only
DWi-Fi → Password → Antivirus → Done
Answer: A - Sensors collect data, networks carry it, systems analyse it, then actions follow.

Q3: Which is a major IoT security risk?

ADevices being too colourful
BUsing too much paper
CDefault passwords and unpatched firmware
DHaving too few sensors
Answer: C - Weak credentials and outdated software leave devices open to attack.
