USB Attack Awareness

Why unknown USB devices are risky

OCN NI Level 5 — Cyber Security Demo

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Explain why found or gifted USBs are dangerous
  • Describe HID/Rubber Ducky style risks at awareness level
  • List organisational controls for removable media
  • Apply a personal “don’t plug it in” rule
  • Report suspicious devices through the right channel

Ethics & Scope

This is an awareness demo. We do not run attack payloads. Understanding the risk helps you defend people and systems.

Authorised use only

Only use tools on systems and networks you are authorised to test.

It Looks Harmless

A USB stick can carry malware — or pretend to be a keyboard and type dangerous commands at superhuman speed. If you didn’t expect the device, don’t plug it in.

Tip

Social engineering often delivers the hardware: “Free USB” at events, “lost” sticks in car parks.

Demo Sequence (safe)

  1. Show awareness props (inert/demo devices) — label “DO NOT CONNECT”
  2. Walk through a baiting scenario storyboard
  3. Explain HID concept at high level (types like a keyboard)
  4. Review organisational USB policy: allowlist, encryption, ports locked
  5. Demonstrate reporting: bag it, tell IT/security, don’t investigate alone
  6. Contrast with approved encrypted corporate media procedures

AOnly charge phones
BCan act like a keyboard and send rapid input
CImprove Wi‑Fi channels automatically
