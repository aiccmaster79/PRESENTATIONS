Anatomy of a CV

Contact Details

Name, phone, email, general location

Personal Statement

2–3 sentences summing up who you are

Education

Most recent first, with dates

Work Experience

Jobs, volunteering, work placements

Skills

Technical and personal skills that fit the role

References

"Available on request" is fine at Level 2

Formatting for Consistency

Inconsistent (Avoid)

  • Different fonts on every heading
  • Random text sizes and colours
  • Mismatched bullet styles
  • Uneven spacing between sections
  • Left, centred and right text mixed together

Consistent (Do)

  • One font family throughout
  • A clear heading style repeated for each section
  • Matching bullet points and spacing
  • Same margins and alignment on every page
  • One page, easy to scan quickly

Common CV Mistakes

Typos & Spelling

Always run a spellcheck and proofread aloud

Unprofessional Email

Use a sensible address, not a nickname

Too Long / Cluttered

Level 2 CVs should fit on one page

Not Tailored

Match your skills to the job you want

Practical Task

Build Your One-Page CV

Using a word processor, create a formatted, one-page CV using your own real (or realistic) details, then export it as a PDF.

  1. Set up the page: A4, sensible margins, one font family
  2. Add a heading with your name and contact details
  3. Write a 2–3 sentence personal statement
  4. List education with dates, most recent first
  5. List experience or volunteering with brief bullet points
  6. Add a skills section relevant to the role
  7. Apply consistent heading styles throughout
  8. Proofread, then export to PDF and check it opens correctly
