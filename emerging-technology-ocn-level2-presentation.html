Emerging Technology

Unit Overview — Understanding Change in IT & Society

OCN NI Level 2 — Understanding Emerging Technology (A/651/0447)

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Define what emerging technology means
  • Identify key emerging technologies (AI, 5G, IoT, Blockchain, Quantum, AR/VR, Biotech)
  • Explain factors that influence business adoption
  • Evaluate impacts on society and business
  • Apply assessment tips for AC 1.1, 2.1, 3.1 and 3.2

What is Emerging Technology?

Emerging technologies are new or rapidly developing tools and systems that are starting to change how people live, learn and work — but are not yet fully mainstream everywhere.

New / Evolving

Still developing quickly; capabilities improve year on year

High Potential

Could transform industries, services and everyday life

Uncertainty

Risks, costs, skills and regulations are still settling

Remember

What is "emerging" today (e.g. generative AI) may become "standard" tomorrow — just like smartphones once were emerging.

Key Emerging Technologies

AI / ML

Systems that learn from data to predict, recommend and automate

5G

Faster mobile networks enabling IoT, AR and low-latency apps

Blockchain

Shared ledgers for secure, transparent transactions and records

IoT

Connected sensors and devices that collect and share data

Quantum

New computing models for ultra-complex problems

AR / VR

Immersive digital experiences for training, retail and design

Biotech

Gene editing, diagnostics and bio-based innovation (e.g. CRISPR)

Business Adoption Factors (1)

Organisations decide whether to adopt emerging tech using these factors:

Strategic Alignment

Does it support business goals, vision and competitive strategy?

Cost–Benefit

Are expected gains worth the purchase, setup and running costs?

Scalability

Can it grow with more users, data and locations?

Integration

Will it work with existing systems, data and processes?

Security & Compliance

Does it meet legal, privacy and cyber security requirements?

Business Adoption Factors (2)

Skills & Training

Do staff have (or can they learn) the skills to use it well?

Capacity to Change

Is the culture ready? Can processes and roles adapt?

Vendor Reliability

Is the supplier stable, supportive and future-proof?

Regulatory Considerations

Do laws, standards or sector rules allow or restrict use?

Assessment tip

Impact on Society

Quality of Life

Healthcare, accessibility and convenience improvements

Connectivity

5G and IoT link people, places and services in new ways

Education

Personalised learning, AR/VR training and remote access

Sustainability

Smart energy, greener supply chains — or higher e-waste risk

Privacy & Ethics

Surveillance, bias, consent and data ownership concerns

Jobs & Inequality

New roles appear; some jobs change; digital divide can widen

Impact on Business

Productivity

Automation and analytics speed up decisions and reduce manual work

Competitiveness

Early adopters can offer better products, prices or customer experience

Structures

Teams, roles and workflows redesign around digital platforms and data

Skills

Demand rises for digital, cyber, data and change-management skills

Assessment Tips: AC 1.1 & 2.1

AC 1.1

  • Describe emerging technologies clearly
  • Use examples (AI, IoT, 5G, CRISPR…)
  • Explain what makes them "emerging"
  • Show they are new/evolving with impact

AC 2.1

  • Link adoption to business factors
  • Cover cost, skills, security, strategy…
  • Use a realistic organisation example
  • Explain why factors help or block adoption

Assessment: AC 3.1, 3.2 & Higher Grades

AC 3.1 — Society

Discuss quality of life, connectivity, education, sustainability, privacy/ethics, jobs

AC 3.2 — Business

Cover productivity, competitiveness, organisational structures and skills needs

Merit

Compare technologies or impacts; give reasoned examples with some analysis

Distinction

Evaluate critically — weigh pros/cons, short vs long term, winners and losers

Write like an evaluator

Pass describes. Merit analyses. Distinction judges significance and justifies conclusions with evidence.

Worked Example: Retail + AI

A supermarket considering AI shelf cameras

Adoption factors

  • Strategic: reduce waste & stockouts
  • Cost–benefit: cameras vs labour savings
  • Skills: train staff to use dashboards
  • Privacy/compliance: GDPR & CCTV rules

Impacts

  • Society: convenience vs surveillance worry
  • Business: productivity up; roles change
  • Skills: demand for data analysts
  • Ethics: customer consent and fairness

Test Your Knowledge!

Quick check on emerging technology concepts for A/651/0447

Q1: What best describes an emerging technology?

AAny tool that has existed for 50+ years unchanged
BA new or rapidly developing technology with growing impact
COnly technologies used by government
DSoftware that never needs updates
Answer: B - Emerging tech is new/evolving and starting to transform society and business.

Q2: Which is a business adoption factor from this unit?

AFavourite colour of the CEO
BScalability and integration with existing systems
CWhether the logo is purple
DNumber of office plants
Answer: B - Scalability, integration, cost–benefit, skills, security and more guide adoption.

Q3: For distinction-level work you should mainly:

AOnly list definitions with no examples
BCopy marketing slogans from vendors
CEvaluate impacts critically with justified conclusions
DAvoid mentioning risks or limitations
Answer: C - Distinction expects evaluation: weigh benefits, risks and significance with evidence.
