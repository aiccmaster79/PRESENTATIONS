Data Protection Basics (GDPR)

What GDPR Protects

GDPR / UK data protection law controls how organisations collect, store, use and share personal data about identifiable people.

Purpose Limitation

Data can only be used for the reason it was collected

Data Minimisation

Collect only what's actually needed

Right of Access

People can ask what data is held about them

Copyright Essentials

Copyright automatically protects original creative work — text, images, music, video and code — the moment it's created.

Usually Needs Permission

  • Copying images from the web into your own work
  • Using music in a video without a licence
  • Sharing purchased software with others

Usually Fine

  • Using content explicitly licensed for reuse
  • Creating your own original work
  • Limited quoting with clear credit (fair dealing)

Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

You, the User

Acceptable Use Policy

College / Employer Systems

Typical AUP Rules

  • No sharing of accounts or passwords
  • No installing unauthorised software
  • No accessing or sharing inappropriate content
  • Systems are for approved study/work purposes

Consequences of Misuse

Account Suspension

Loss of system access for AUP breaches

Fines

Organisations can be fined heavily for data breaches

Job / Course Loss

Serious misuse can lead to dismissal or expulsion

Legal Action

Copyright infringement and data breaches can lead to court action

Judging a Scenario

Ask Yourself...

  • Does this involve someone's personal data? Is there a lawful reason to use it?
  • Does this use someone else's creative work without permission or a licence?
  • Does this break the organisation's Acceptable Use Policy?
  • Would this cause harm or unfairness to someone else?

Rule of Thumb

If in doubt, ask permission, check the licence, or ask your tutor/IT department before you act.

Practical Task

Is This Allowed?

  • In teams, work through 6 short scenario cards
  • For each, decide: Allowed or Not Allowed
  • Justify your verdict against GDPR, copyright, or the college AUP
  • Present one scenario and your reasoning to the class
  • Compare verdicts and discuss any disagreements

Stay Safe

All scenarios are fictional teaching examples for discussion — not real names or real incidents.

Q1: GDPR mainly regulates...

AWi-Fi router settings
BHow personal data is collected, stored and used
CSoftware licensing fees only
DAntivirus installation
Answer: B - GDPR/UK data protection law governs the handling of personal data.

Q2: Using an image found online in your own project without checking its licence is...

AAlways completely fine
BRisky — it may breach copyright
CRequired by law
DOnly a problem for videos, not images
Answer: B - Most online images are copyrighted; always check the licence before reuse.

Q3: An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) mainly sets out...

AThe colour scheme of the website
BRules for how you may use an organisation's IT systems
CRouter firmware update schedules
DCopyright licensing fees
Answer: B - The AUP defines what is and isn't allowed when using an organisation's systems.

Key Takeaways

Protect Personal Data

Only collect and use what's necessary and lawful

Respect Copyright

Check licences before reusing others' work

Follow the AUP

Every organisation's systems have rules — know them

Misuse Has Consequences

Fines, dismissal or legal action are all possible
